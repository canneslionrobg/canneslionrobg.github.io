Seasonal Heat Map

When creating media plans and forecasts it is important to be able to distinguish between trends that are seasonal vs those that are one-offs. This can be difficult and time-consuming when trawling back through previous years’ data. The circular heat map is designed to display several years data in one place, making spotting what are seasonal trends a much simpler task.

The above graph, created in javascript using the D3 package, was produced with the previous 4 years of revenue data from a luxury makeup brand. The data for each year was normalised to be a percentage of the largest value for that year; this was done so that we can specifically focus on seasonal trends throughout a year, this is of course optional and if you wanted to also view YoY trends you could simply plot the actual values rather than a percentage.

The above example illustrates the issue with merely looking at the previous year’s data when predicting the future. We can see that July was the best month for revenue, however the heatmap indicates that this was a one-off rather than the norm. The general trend in the past couple of years appears to be that November has replaced December as the best performing month – not a surprise with the rise in popularity of Black Friday in the UK.

The strength of this new chart idea lies in its simplicity. The ability to quickly determine what is a seasonal trend vs a one-off is a great tool in an analyst armoury, and this can now be achieved much faster.

Quality Score Audit

Quality Score and CPC bid are the two components that determine where an ad appears in an auction. A high quality score is vital when trying to improve average position and lower CPCs. Despite this very little insight was previously available for this metric.

In March 2016 a new AdWords API was released enabling a breakdown of a keywords quality score to be retrieved at scale for the first time. With a detailed quality score report being unavailable in AdWords or any bid management platform such as Kenshoo or DoubleClick, I developed an innovative in-house solution using an AdWords Script that can be applied to any PPC account, and on up to 50 accounts from a single script.

These previously unavailable insights have led to considerable gains in quality score, which ultimately led to a YoY reduction in CPCs of 42% on the La Roche-Posay account. The script also won our internal eXcellence award for ‘Best Innovation’ in 2016.

Live Revenue Tracker

    The Black Friday sale period has grown dramatically over the last few years in the UK, becoming the biggest revenue-generating period for many businesses in the country. L’Oréal’s luxury division are no exception to this, and with over 10% of their revenue in 2015 coming from the Black Friday/Cyber Monday weekend, there was a huge emphasis on the same period in 2016.

    With so much attention being placed on a single weekend, and so many stakeholders involved due to the size of the brands, an opportunity was identified to develop something new and different that could set iCrossing apart from the other agencies L’Oréal work with.

    Using Google Sheets I created a script that populated a dashboard, updating every 5 minutes with the latest sales data from Google Analytics. Key metrics were called out, such as SEO and PPC performance vs last year, to highlight any areas that were over/under performing. A graph showing minute-by-minute sales vs last year allowed performance to be tracked throughout the day.

    Whilst the live revenue tracker could have some practical benefits in spotting underperforming areas early, it was mainly designed as a bit of fun for Black Friday. It acts as an example of iCrossing’s innovation and helps us stand out against rival agencies.

AdWords Scripts

AdWords scripts provide a way to programmatically control your AdWords data using JavaScript in a browser-based IDE. Common tasks can be automated to save analyst time for higher value tasks and strategic planning.

As the UKs go-to expert for AdWords scripts I have so far created the following:

  • Automated trackers – save 20mins per day by automatically pulling in performance data
  • Negative exact match mirror – saves 10mins per upload by automatically applying negative mirrors. Is also more accurate as it updates daily & is applied to all BMM campaigns through the use of a shared list.
  • PLA Non-Brand negatives – saves 10mins per day by automatically applying non-brand negatives to a PLA campaign. Increases performance by maximising budget spent on brand.
  • Quality Score Audit – automatically produces a QS report that would take around 1 hour to produce manually.
  • Out of stock product pauser – uses the PLA feed to automatically pause keywords directing to a product that is out of stock. Saves 20mins per day vs doing this manually.

Sunburst Chart

Sunburst diagrams are ideal for displaying hierarchical data, this makes them a great way to visualise an AdWords account. The above chart has been created in javascript using the D3 package and is interactive, whereby selecting a node will allow you to focus in on that particular entity.

In the above example, each ring corresponds to a level in the account hierarchy, with the central root representing each campaign within the account. Outside of this you then have the ad groups and finally each keyword.

Visualising an account in this way can allow you to gain a greater understanding of how important certain keywords are to your account. It can be used to show clients how many clicks, or how much revenue, certain keywords are driving relative to the rest of the account. This understanding can be invaluable when working with a client to prioritise areas to optimise.
